# **GP – Gold Master Release** **Enceladus Expedition**

## **GAME DESCRIPTION**

In Enceladus Expedition, you are the cybernetic captain of a cargo ship navigating the treacherous oceans of Saturn's moon. Battle terrifying sea creatures, manage your ship's energy, and survive solar radiation as you deliver vital supplies to terraform Enceladus, all while unraveling the secrets of humanity's last hope.

## **ENCELADUS EXPEDITION GAME DESIGN DOCUMENT**

1. ### **Characters**

You start as the captain of a small ship delivering cargo supplies on one of Saturn’s moons, Enceladus. You are mostly human but part machine, and survive off of electrical power instead of food.

Sea creatures are the other characters in this game, and come in all types of sizes. Not all are ones we’ve seen on Earth, and very few are exactly as we remember from Ancient Earth. They have a vast range of sizes and forms ranging from humanoid to alien-like. Most of them are aggressive to the player.

2. ### **Story/Narrative**

The year is somewhere around 5,000,000,000, and the sun has started expanding. Ancient Earth has become uninhabitable, and humans are attempting to settle on one of Saturn’s moons, Enceladus. Enceladus was an icy ocean moon before the sun started expanding, and has since melted. Humans are in the process of terraforming this moon to make it resemble Ancient Earth’s environment. Your job is to aid in this effort by delivering cargo between island ports on Enceladus. If you survive long enough, you may live to see Enceladus become as inhabitable as Ancient Earth was, although we know this can only be temporary. The ever-looming death of the sun will eventually force humans to new frontiers, unless you can find a way to stop it.

3. ### **Game World**

You spend most of your time on your cargo ship sailing on Enceladus, a vast ocean moon orbiting Saturn. The atmosphere isn’t fully breathable for humans yet, hence the suits the user must wear. Solar radiation is unsafe in the day, which means travel is only possible at night. The ocean is full of all sorts of creatures, both indigenous to Earth due to the terraforming process, and new species that have evolved to adapt to Enceladus’ environment. You may also discover points of interest, like islands, shipwrecks, and more, which could contain valuable loot and materials. Bunkers are located around the moon, and are storage places for vital resources that you’re tasked with delivering

4. ### **Gameplay**

You are the captain of your ship, tasked with protecting it from hostile creatures, avoiding treacherous obstacles, and defending the ship to bring as much supplies as possible. You can strengthen yourself with the shop aboard the ship, where you can buy stronger weapons to defend yourself. Enceladus Expedition will play similarly to a wave-based PvE FPS with progress-based elements.

5. ### **Game Mechanics**

Player Mechanics:

- Maintain your energy levels. Humans haven’t needed to eat since ancient times, but all animate things required power  
- Collect meat dropped by creatures  
- Fend off smaller enemies by meleeing them or attacking them with your weapons  
- Acquire stronger weapons (shotgun, rifle, sniper) to kill creatures more efficiently

Ship Mechanics:

- Maintain power levels of your ship  
  - Avoid overusing the healing center that depletes energy  
  - Recharge ship by incinerating monster meat dropped by enemies  
  - Can speed up ship timer by standing next to steering wheel, will deplete more energy  
  - At zero energy the ship stops moving

NPC Mechanics

- Robots patrol the area to provide guidance for the player  
  - Can interact with robots by pressing E, will then talk to the player  
  - Are always patrolling certain sections  
- Monsters will jump out from the ocean to attack the player  
  - They have a keen sense of smell, and will always be able to find the player  
    - Brutes have much higher hitpoints, but slower attacks  
    - Zlorps are smaller, faster, but less durable  
  - Are very mobile, and can jump from high heights  
  - Drop meat which can be used to power the ship and get better weapons  
- Flying enemies (watchers)  
  - Will idle in the same spot when player is out of range  
  - Will chase the player when close enough and shoot a fireball when in range

6. ### **Items, Loots, and Power-ups**

Shop system

- In the beginning, the player will start off with a pistol to defend themselves, which deals 10 damage  
- Every monster that is killed can drop a monster meat, which can be used to either charge the incinerator or buy a new weapon  
  - Charging the incinerator makes it so you can use energy to heal yourself  
  - Getting enough meat to purchase a weapon gives you the ability to deal more damage to monsters  
- The shop contains 3 weapons: Rifle, Shotgun, Sniper  
  - Rifle: deals 10 additional damage to the enemies  
  - Shotgun: deals 15 additional damage to the enemies  
  - Sniper: deals 20 additional damage to the enemies  
- Shop is activated if player is close enough and presses E

7. ### **Game Rules**

Winning:

- 3 Primary win conditions:  
  - Charging ship to 100%  
    - This is the condition for the first level, in which you set sail from the dock  
  - Surviving the timer  
    - This is the condition for the second level where you defend the ship from enemies attacking you as you transport items  
  - Transporting loot  
    - This is the condition for the third level, where you bring the precious cargo from the ship to the bunker

Losing:

- Dying:  
  - Anytime your health reaches zero, you lose the level.  
  - When the player dies, all collected meat will be lost and the player will start again with a pistol  
  -  This can be caused by mainly 2 things:  
    - Monsters overwhelm you and kill you  
    - You fall into the ocean (electricity and water don’t mix well)

8. ### **Target Audience**

The target audience for this game are people who like the apocalyptic type of game environments with a large emphasis on progression, and also for people who enjoy a bit of horror. Our target age range during our development would be the teen to adult range, but the game itself wouldn’t have any hard age limits.
